I am a PhD Researcher in Mathematical Analysis, Modeling and Applications at SISSA, Trieste, Italy, with a background in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering. My work lies at the intersection of computational mechanics, applied mathematics, scientific computing, and engineering simulation.

My research focuses on high-fidelity and reduced-order modeling for parametric partial differential equation systems, with applications to plate and shell mechanics, thermomechanical models, multiphysics simulations, uncertainty quantification, optimization, and engineering digital twins. I work with projection-based and data-driven reduced-order models, including POD-Galerkin methods, neural-network surrogates, autoencoder-based latent models, and scientific machine learning approaches for fast many-query simulation.

I also have experience in CFD, FEA, aeroacoustics, turbulent flow modeling, system identification, physics-based dynamic modeling, validation workflows, and scientific software development. My technical background includes Python, C++, MATLAB, FEniCS, RBniCS, EZyRB, PyTorch, Scikit-learn, OpenFOAM, AVBP, ANSYS, Abaqus, COMSOL, FreeFEM++, deal.II, and related scientific computing and engineering simulation tools.

## Research interests

- Computational mechanics and finite element methods
- Reduced-order modeling for parametric PDE systems
- Scientific machine learning and surrogate modeling
- Plate and shell mechanics, thermomechanical modeling, and multiphysics simulation
- CFD/FEA workflows, optimization, uncertainty quantification, and engineering digital twins
