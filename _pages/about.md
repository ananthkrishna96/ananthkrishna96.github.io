# Anantha Krishnan Orunnukaran Mani

I am a PhD Researcher in Mathematical Analysis, Modeling and Applications at the Scuola Internazionale Superiore di Studi Avanzati (SISSA), Trieste, Italy. My background is in mechanical and aerospace engineering, and my current work lies at the intersection of computational mechanics, applied mathematics, scientific computing, and engineering simulation.

My research focuses on high-fidelity and reduced-order modeling for parametric partial differential equation systems, with applications to orthotropic plate and shell mechanics, thermomechanical modeling, multiphysics simulation, uncertainty quantification, optimization, and engineering digital twins. I work with projection-based, intrusive, and non-intrusive reduced-order modeling techniques, including POD-Galerkin methods, data-driven surrogate models, neural-network surrogates, autoencoder latent models, and machine-learning-based coefficient prediction for fast many-query simulation.

I also have experience in CFD, FEA, aeroacoustics, turbulent-flow modeling, system identification, solar-thermal dynamic modeling, validation with operational data, and scientific software development. My technical background includes Python, C++, MATLAB, FEniCS, RBniCS, EZyRB, PyTorch, Scikit-learn, OpenFOAM, AVBP, ANSYS, Abaqus, COMSOL, FreeFEM++, deal.II, and related scientific computing and engineering simulation tools.
