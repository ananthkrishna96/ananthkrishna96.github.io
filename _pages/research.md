My research combines computational mechanics, applied mathematics, numerical methods, reduced-order modeling, and scientific machine learning for efficient simulation of complex engineering systems.

## Computational mechanics and finite element modeling

I develop and use high-fidelity numerical models for structural and multiphysics problems, with emphasis on finite element methods for plate and shell systems. My current PhD work focuses on fourth-order finite element models for orthotropic plates and shells on elastic foundations, including unilateral contact, panel-interface coupling, thermomechanical extensions, transient dynamic response prediction, coupled simulation, verification, and ROM-oriented workflows.

## Reduced-order modeling for parametric PDE systems

A central part of my research is the construction of reduced-order models for fast and reliable many-query simulation. I work with projection-based, intrusive, and non-intrusive ROM techniques, including POD-Galerkin projection, POD-NN models, neural-network-based coefficient prediction, data-driven surrogate models, and autoencoder latent representations.

These methods are relevant for design exploration, optimization, uncertainty quantification, and real-time or near-real-time simulation of parameter-dependent engineering systems.

## Scientific machine learning and surrogate modeling

I am interested in scientific machine learning methods that combine physics-based numerical modeling with data-driven approximation. My work includes neural-network surrogates, autoencoder-based latent-space models, ML-assisted ROMs, and reduced models for parametric PDE systems.

My broader goal is to build computational workflows that reduce the cost of high-fidelity simulation while preserving physical interpretability, numerical reliability, and engineering usefulness.

## Multiphysics simulation and engineering digital twins

My research interests also include thermomechanical coupling, CFD/FEA simulation workflows, uncertainty quantification, optimization, and engineering digital twins. I am particularly interested in methods that make high-fidelity simulation usable in many-query contexts such as design optimization, parameter studies, control-oriented models, and real-time prediction.

## CFD, aeroacoustics, and system identification

My earlier research work involved CFD-based characterization of aeroacoustic transfer matrices for narrow ducts and perforated plates, using compressible CFD, broadband forcing, post-processing, system identification, and low-order acoustic network modeling. This work connects CFD, ROM, data analysis, and computational efficiency for acoustic and flow systems.

## Solar-thermal dynamic modeling

During my research internship at AEE INTEC, I developed physics-based solar-thermal models within the SunPeek D-CAT framework, including collector-field dynamics, 1D pipe heat-loss models, lumped 0D heat-exchanger models, validation with operational data, sensor mapping, automated tests, and diagnostic plots.
