This master's thesis studied the characterization of aeroacoustic transfer matrices for narrow ducts and perforated plates based on compressible Computational Fluid Dynamics and System Identification.

The work connected CFD simulation, acoustic excitation, data analysis, low-order acoustic network modeling, and computationally efficient reduced representations for aeroacoustic systems.
