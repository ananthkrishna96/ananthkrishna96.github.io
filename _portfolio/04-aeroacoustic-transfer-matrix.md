This master's thesis project studied the characterization of aeroacoustic transfer matrices for narrow ducts and perforated plates using compressible Computational Fluid Dynamics and System Identification.

The work included CFD simulations of steady and acoustically excited duct/pipe flows, broadband forcing, boundary treatments, post-processing, data analysis, and development of low-order acoustic network models to improve computational efficiency.

**Keywords:** CFD, aeroacoustics, AVBP, system identification, turbulent flows, reduced-order modeling, MATLAB.
